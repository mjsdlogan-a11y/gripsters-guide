On the Road / Switzerland
Central Europe · Three weeks

Slow trains &
sharp peaks

By The Gripsters · June 2025 · 12 min read
🏔️ Alpine 🚂 Rail 🧀 Food

Nobody told us the cheese fondue in Gruyères would be this good. Or that the 7:14am train from Interlaken to Lauterbrunnen, mist still clinging to the valley floor, would be the single best hour of the entire trip. Switzerland kept surprising us — and we'd done our research.

We'd been told Switzerland was expensive, efficient, and beautiful. Two out of three of those things turned out to be understatements. The third — the cost — was entirely manageable once we stopped treating it like a budget destination and leaned into what Switzerland actually does best: extraordinary train travel, extraordinary food, and views that make you go quiet.

This is the story of three weeks moving through a country that rewards slowness. No hire car, no group tour, no ticking off a checklist. Just a Swiss Travel Pass, a very good pair of walking shoes, and the willingness to sit on a train for an hour just because the view out the window was worth it.

Arrival: Zürich, unhurried

We flew into Zürich and gave ourselves two full days before touching a single mountain. This turned out to be exactly right. The city rewards slow walking: the old town (Altstadt) on both banks of the Limmat river, the guild houses, the paired churches of Grossmünster and Fraumünster facing each other across the water.
